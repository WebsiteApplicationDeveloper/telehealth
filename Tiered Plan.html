Strategy & Overview Product & Market Fit

The MVP is built to test local demand density, partner willingness to compete on price, and repeat consumer usage — without over-engineering the stack.

Goals & Objectives

Why this product, right now.

  • Enable customers to discover the best local price within minutes.
  • Provide small retailers with a digital lead-generation channel.
  • Capture structured data on demand, pricing, and product categories.
  • Lay the foundation for a subscription-based B2B revenue model.

Key Needs

What the MVP must prove.

  • Frictionless quote submission from non-technical users.
  • Fast, intuitive experience that encourages daily app usage.
  • Reliable notifications and turnaround time from stores.
  • Clear pricing visibility and fair competition between suppliers.

Market Trends

Signals supporting the model.

  • Rising comfort with mobile-first local commerce.
  • Price-sensitive consumers increasingly comparing offers.
  • Local retailers seeking measurable digital channels.
  • Shift from “scroll to discover” to “request and receive”.

Target Audience Demand & Supply Sides

The solution aligns two audiences: time-conscious, price-aware consumers and local retailers eager for predictable, trackable demand.

Customer Demographics

  • Age 22–55, mobile-first, urban and suburban residents.
  • Price-sensitive, but care about convenience and time saved.
  • Comfortable sharing product photos and simple descriptions.

Retailer Segments

  • Electronics, appliances, furniture, auto parts, and services.
  • Local independents and small chains within defined city radius.
  • Digitally curious, willing to respond to leads via mobile.

Personas

  • “Smart Saver Sam” – compares offers before every big purchase.
  • “Busy Parent Priya” – values quick decisions over visiting multiple stores.
  • “Store Owner Omar” – wants leads that convert, not just impressions.

Technical Details Stack & Architecture

The MVP uses a modern, secure stack engineered for smooth Android performance, scalable APIs, and Play Store compliance from the first release.

Technology Stack

  • Android app in Kotlin / Java with Material Design 3.
  • Secure backend (e.g., Node.js or PHP/Laravel) with REST APIs.
  • Cloud database (e.g., PostgreSQL / MySQL) + object storage for images.
  • Firebase Authentication & Push Notifications.

Key Integrations

  • Google Play Billing for business subscriptions (phase-ready).
  • Geolocation / Maps SDK for nearest-store logic.
  • Analytics events for requests, responses, and engagement.
  • Privacy policy and consent flows embedded in-app.

Compliance & Security

  • Explicit permission prompts with usage explanation.
  • Encrypted transport (HTTPS) for all API calls.
  • Role-based access for customers, businesses, and admins.
  • Data export and deletion options to support privacy laws.
Client Layer
Android App (Customers & Businesses)

Handles UI, form validation, offline-friendly queues, and secure auth tokens.

Application Layer
API Gateway & Business Logic

Manages quote routing, notification triggers, subscriptions, and admin configuration.

Data & Services
Database, Storage & Analytics

Stores requests, quotes, store profiles, usage metrics, and supports reporting & ROI tracking.

Feature Priority MVP Matrix

The MVP focuses on must-have experience drivers; additional layers like advanced billing and in-depth analytics are lined up for future phases.

Must-have MVP v1
  • Customer registration & profile management.
  • Business onboarding, basic profile & category setup.
  • Product request with description, photos, and geo range.
  • Quote submission by stores + notifications.
  • Store search by name, category, and proximity.
  • Admin approval for businesses and content visibility.
  • Privacy policy and consent screens for Play Store.
Should-have Phase 1.5
  • In-app store rating and basic feedback tagging.
  • Saved favourite stores for faster repeat requests.
  • Basic subscription tracking for business trials.
  • Request history and simple analytics for users.
  • Category-based curated “Top Stores” views.
Nice-to-have Future
  • Real-time chat between customers and stores.
  • Advanced revenue analytics & cohort reports.
  • Dynamic pricing rules and regional promos.
  • Multi-city onboarding with per-city pricing.
  • AI-assisted request templates and suggestions.

SWOT Summary

SStrengths

Hyper-relevant offers, highly engaging request flow, and strong value for local retailers.

WWeaknesses

OOpportunities

Expansion into services, premium placements, and data-driven insights for retailers.

TThreats

Large marketplaces adding similar flows, and local players copying the quote mechanism.
